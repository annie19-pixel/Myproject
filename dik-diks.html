Dik-diks

The dik-dik is a type of dwarf antelope that lives in arid regions of East Africa.They can
live in such barren environments because they get water from the vegetation they eat.
They also take cover in undergrowth to protect themselves from predators, including eagles and cats.

There are four species of dik-diks, but just two of them live in Kenya. These include
Guenthers's dik-dik and kirk's dik-dik. Kirk's is the largest dik-dik, though it's still small
in comparison to other antelopes. It stands at just 45 centimeters(18 inches) tall and
weighs only 7.2.kilograms (15.8)pounds. Guenther's dik-dik is the samllest but has an longer snout.

The IUCN lists both Kirk's and Guenther's dik-diks as leat concern, though they are
threatened by expanding agricultural settlements and exessive hunting in soem areas.
