Black-backed jackals

The black-backed jackal is a member of the dog family. It has a rusty red coat with a
black patch on its back and a black-tipped tail. It measure around 95 centimeters(37
inches) from head to tail.

As opportunistic omnivores, jackals eat they can. They work in family packs to
hunt small antelopes, but they'll also feed on reptiles, insects, birds, fruits, berries, and
carcasses left by larger predators.

Ther are two distinct populations of black-backed jackals in Africa-one in East africa
, in countries including Kenya, Ethiopia, and Tanzania, and one in southern Africa, in
counries including Namibia, Angola, and South Africa.

These animals live in a wide variety of habitats, from deserts to grasslands to savannahs.
The IUCN lists black-backed jackals as least concern, meaning they face few threats.
However they are hunted by farmers who kill then to protect their livestock.
